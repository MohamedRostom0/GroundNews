Germany's Christian Democratic Union leader Friedrich Merz. Photo: Reuters
Germany’s main opposition leader vowed yesterday to impose immediate border controls if he becomes chancellor, as expected, after elections next month, a day after an Afghan asylum-seeker was arrested for a deadly knife attack targeting children.
Friedrich Merz – whose plan would need buy-in from coalition partners after the February 23 vote his party is set to win with a minority – said the attack, which killed a two-year-old boy and an adult passer-by, could not become the new normal.
